Significant Outcomes (Matched)

Key Findings (After Propensity Score Matching)

  • Open CTR had 3.1× higher odds of hospital admission (95% CI: 1.6–5.9)
  • Open CTR had 2.2× higher odds of superficial SSI (95% CI: 1.7–2.8)
  • Open CTR had 2.2× higher odds of median nerve injury (95% CI: 1.0–4.6)
  • Open CTR had 1.2× higher odds of ED visits (95% CI: 1.1–1.4)

Forest plot showing odds ratios for each 90-day complication after propensity score matching. Diamond markers to the right of the dashed line (OR = 1) indicate higher risk with open CTR. Red = statistically significant (P < .05); gray = not significant.

Side-by-side complication rates after matching. Asterisks (*) mark outcomes with statistically significant differences.

How propensity score matching affected the odds ratios. Purple = unmatched (raw), green = matched. Matching adjusts for demographic and comorbidity differences between groups.

Absolute risk difference (percentage points) and Number Needed to Treat (NNT) for matched cohorts. NNT represents how many patients would need to receive ECTR instead of OCTR to prevent one additional complication.

Comorbidity prevalence in the matched cohorts. After matching, several comorbidities were actually more prevalent in the ECTR group, suggesting the observed benefits of ECTR may be even more robust.

Sex breakdown across all four cohorts (unmatched and matched). After propensity score matching the sex distribution is nearly identical between OCTR and ECTR groups, confirming successful covariate balance.

Race/ethnicity prevalence in the matched cohorts. Proportions are very similar between groups after matching, validating that racial composition was balanced. “Other/Unknown” aggregates Other Race, Native Hawaiian, American Indian, and Unknown categories.

Parallel coordinates comparing % female and all five comorbidity rates across all four cohorts. Each axis is independently scaled. Note that after matching, comorbidity rates are actually higher in the ECTR group — suggesting the ECTR benefit is conservative (i.e., more impactful than measured).

Complication rates before propensity score matching, showing the raw (unadjusted) differences between the full open CTR cohort (N = 22,435) and endoscopic CTR cohort (N = 4,947).

Heatmap of 90-day post-operative outcome rates (%) for metacarpal repair surgery, stratified by pre-operative comorbidity group. Darker cells indicate higher rates. Data source: raw TriNetX query (metacarpal repair outcomes Excel file).

Key clinical outcome rates for metacarpal repair by comorbidity group: ED visits, readmission, additional surgery, nerve/vessel injury, and pain at surgical site. Kidney disease and ischemic heart disease groups show notably higher readmission rates.
